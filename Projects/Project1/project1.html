Website Critiques

  1. What was your first impression of this site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information? (Do they have links on the left and text on the right? some other scheme? No scheme?)
  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

Website Critique: Jami Lin - Licensed Esthetician

www.jamilin.com

  1. My first impression of the Jamilin.com website was that it feels very busy and difficult to read. The creator placed bright lighting effects behind the main header text, I feel the creater was trying to enhance the text with this effect, but it made it harder to read. The site is also extremely colorful, which makes it difficult to focus on any single element or message.
  2. The website is organized horizontally, with navigation buttons across the top of the page. I did appreciate that the subpages appear to color-coordinate with their corresponding navigation buttons. However, the homepage also contains images with brief descriptions that link to the same pages already accessible through the top navigation buttons. This feels redundant and does not add much value to the overall flow of information.
  3. The overall structure of the website is confusing because there is so much happening at once. There are numerous images, blocks of text, bright colors, and lighting effects that compete for attention. If the site were simplified and more restrained visually, it would be much easier to navigate and understand.
  4. The images on the site do not enhance the layout or user experience. Most of the images are repeated photos of the website owner’s face, which do not add useful context. Since she is an esthetician, the site would be better served by including before-and-after photos of clients or testimonials that demonstrate her services. Additionally, the color scheme is very distracting and makes it difficult to focus on the content.
  5. Despite the large number of images and graphics, the homepage loads surprisingly fast. It took approximately 3-5 seconds to load, which I believe is reasonable given the amount of visual content on the page.
  6. Overall, the size of the text is readable, but the background lighting behind the banner text blurs the lettering and makes it look fuzzy. Some buttons use dark blue backgrounds with bright yellow text, which is also difficult to read. The Chakra & Color Triumphs page uses too many colors at once, making the page overwhelming and discouraging users to stay. The Galactic Spirits page uses text that is much larger than the rest of the site, which makes it feel as though the page is accidentally zoomed in and requires excessive scrolling.
  7. The homepage does include a navigation bar; however, many links open in new browser tabs. This causes the user to constantly switch between tabs while navigating the site, which feels inconvenient and disruptive to the people trying to navigate the site.
  8. Each page has its own color scheme, and those schemes remain consistent within individual pages. However, the overall look and feel changes significantly from page to page. The design reminds me of a 1990s infomercial, which makes the site feel dated and inconsistent as a whole.
  9. The website actually appears cleaner and less cluttered on a smaller screen. The site prompts users to rotate their phone horizontally for a better experience, I disagree with that suggestion. In portrait mode, the site feels less busy and easier to navigate.
  10. The site offers an unusually wide range of services related to skincare and spiritual wellness. However, these offerings feel scattered and unorganized. It appears that new pages are added as new services are learned, without considering the overall structure or flow of the website. I feel that consolidating similar services would drastically improve both usability and the professionalism of the site.
